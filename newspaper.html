2014 THE WORLD'S HIGHEST-PAID
ATHLETES EARNINGS

By Helen Smith

The New York Yankees' captain announced in February that he would retire after the 2014 season, which marks his 20th year in pinstripes. Jeter signed a one-year deal for $12 million before the season and his farewell tour is a home run for the Yankees and their opponents when the Yanks hit the road. Jeter remains the sport's top pitchman with deals with Nike, Gatorade, Steiner Sports, Movado, Rawlings, 24-hour Fitness and Avon Products. He signed a deal in January with health-food developer and marketer, Luvo. He'll be a partner and Brand Development Officer of the company. Jeter also partnered with Simon & Schuster to publish a series of adult and children's books. The first is set to be released in September 2014. Jeter has also expressed interest in being an owner of a MLB team. Dalton has a solid résumé (it’s pretty unreasonable to hold 2019 against him considering he was surrounded by an abysmal team) and strong arm. Like Newton, he likely could be had for a late-round pick, and his salary is a bit more palatable at $17 million. A restructure and/or extension always is a possibility if Dalton excels early.
